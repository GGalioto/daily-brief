2 · 🎓 Business School — Module 17

Time vs. money leverage: services vs. products vs. platforms

Norm AI's headline is the perfect classroom. Ask why a compliance company is worth $1.2 billion when a compliance consultant is worth their hourly rate, and you've found today's whole lesson: the same underlying work can be sold in three structurally different wrappers, and the wrapper — not the effort — sets your income ceiling.

Services — you sell your hours. Income is capped at hours × rate, and it stops the second you stop working. This is exactly the "locked door" Business Intelligence work you already walked away from: fine as knowledge, a trap as a living.

Products — you do the work once, freeze the result, and sell that frozen result many times. CashMoney (built once, $50 each additional sale ≈ near-pure margin) and the GoHighLevel (GHL) Heating, Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC) Snapshot are your real products. The cost is front-loaded; the Nth copy costs almost nothing (this is Module 4's fixed-vs-variable idea doing its work).

Platforms — other people build and transact on top of you, and you take a cut. Norm AI, GoHighLevel itself, and the ambition behind Anvil live here. Highest leverage, highest build cost, and usually needs a scale you don't have yet.

Worked example — your own 6 hours, three ways:

As a service: 6 × $50 = $300, once.
As the Snapshot product: 6 hours sunk, then $997 × N at ~87–90% margin (Module 7's margin math).
As a platform: a few cents per downstream transaction, forever — but only if the volume ever shows up.

The catch that stops this from being free money: leverage always costs something. Products demand distribution — you still have to find each buyer (Module 16's "first 10" problem). Platforms demand a crowd of other people first. Services need neither, which is precisely why they're the easiest to start and the hardest to escape.

Applied to today's verdict: you are deliberately building Notification & Compliance as a product (one engine, per-state adapters, duplicable to other remediation firms) instead of a service (filing Rainbow's forms by hand forever). That architecture choice is a leverage choice — and it's the right one. The consulting → Anvil pipeline only becomes real leverage if the consulting work gets frozen into reusable product, not re-done per client.
