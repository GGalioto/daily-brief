🎓 2 · Business School — Module 5

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

The one idea: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is the total money and money-equivalent you spend to turn one stranger into one paying customer — averaged across everyone you win, including the people who cost you effort and never bought.

The trap it exists to catch

New founders look at a sale and think "I charged $300, it cost me $97 to deliver, so I made $203." That's contribution margin (Module 3) — real, but it ignores the money you burned to find that customer in the first place. CAC is the number that hides in the "before the sale" darkness. A business with great margins can still bleed to death if it spends $400 in ads to land a customer worth $203.

How you actually calculate it

Add up everything you spent trying to get customers in a period — ad spend, tools, the dollar-value of your own hours, any fees — then divide by the number of customers you actually landed. Note the word landed, not "leads." The people who ghosted you still count in the numerator; that's what makes CAC honest and brutal.

CAC = (all sales & marketing cost) ÷ (new customers won)

Worked example — with your own numbers

Say you spend a Saturday — call it 6 hours of your time, worth $50/hr to you = $300 — doing manual outreach to HVAC shops for the Snapshot. You also pay $0 in ads. Out of it, 2 contractors buy at $497.

LineAmount
Your time (6 hrs × $50)$300
Ad spend$0
Customers won2
CAC (per customer)$150

$150 to acquire a $497 sale is excellent — you keep the difference. But flip one number: if only 1 shop bought, CAC doubles to $300. And if you'd paid $400 in Meta ads and landed 1 customer, your CAC is $700 for a $497 sale — you lost money on that customer even though the "margin" looked fine.

Why this matters this week

Today's real-world proof lands right on top of the lesson. A fresh survey of 3,787 revenue-earning bootstrapped Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) businesses found the ones that win almost all did it with a CAC near $0 — 100% organic and community-driven. One solo founder hit $200,000 Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) in six months with a literal CAC of $0 (every customer came from Reddit and word-of-mouth). Meanwhile the same data shows paid ads are a margin-destroyer at your scale — organic search "costs" $480–$942 per customer in built-up effort, and paid ads run so hot they only make sense for very high Lifetime Value (LTV) products.

The takeaway that changes what you do: Your cheapest, best CAC lever right now is your own hands + free channels (that enriched GHL prospect list, Reddit, GHL agency communities) — not opening your wallet. The instinct to "just run some ads" is the expensive amateur move. Bank the free CAC while you're small.
